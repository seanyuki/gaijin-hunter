What this means for foreigners job-hunting in Japan

Three forces are stacked in foreign candidates' favour right now:

  • Bilinguals command premium offers. Companies entering Japan and Japanese firms going global both compete intensely for English+Japanese talent. Job-changer raises of 10–20% are now normal, up to 30% for cybersecurity, AI, M&A, and HSP-qualifying profiles.
  • Visa-sponsorship reluctance is fading. Foreign-resident count has nearly doubled in a decade. Some multinationals are now hiring engineers and infrastructure specialists without requiring Japanese.
  • Flexible work is a deal-breaker. Employers without remote/hybrid options are losing top candidates regardless of base pay. Verify policy before signing.

Methodology & how to read these numbers

What's in the data

Industry-wide 2026 forecast salaries are aggregated from permanent placements made across major recruitment disciplines in 2025 plus forward-looking projections. Each number is the advertised salary range (basic salary only, excluding bonuses and benefits unless noted). Live percentile data is computed from the actual postings indexed on this job board (last 30 days), using the midpoint of each posting's disclosed range.

Reading the ranges

  • Permanent salary: annual gross JPY before tax. Most Japanese contracts quote 年収 (nenshu), annual base + bonus. Some employers list base only; always confirm.
  • Contract rates: hourly JPY for fixed-term / temp staffing. Multiply by ~2000 hours/yr for rough annual equivalent (no bonus, no benefits).
  • Years: minimum experience expected, not a hard floor. Strong candidates routinely slot in below.
  • Where you'll land: for first-time candidates without leverage, expect the bottom 1/3 of the range. For experienced bilinguals or in-demand specialists, mid-to-top.

How to use this with the rest of the site

  • Live percentile data tells you what the market is actually paying this month.
  • Offer comparator shows where a specific offer falls in the distribution.
  • Take-home pay calculator converts any gross JPY into estimated net after Japan's tax + social-insurance burden (~25–30% for typical earners).
  • Cost-of-living comparator compares a Japan offer against your current city for purchasing power.
  • Negotiation playbook shows how to ask for 10–20% above the offer without burning the bridge.
