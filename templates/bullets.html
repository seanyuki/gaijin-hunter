The STAR / CAR pattern

Every strong bullet has these moving parts. Most weak bullets are missing the metric and the result.

Action
Verb in past tense, what YOU did.
Object
The specific thing, system, team, feature, customer segment.
Metric
Numbers anchor credibility: ¥, %, ms, users, tx/day.
Result
The business outcome you produced.

Strong action verbs

Drop in for "Helped," "Worked on," "Responsible for," "Involved in."

Built created from scratch
Led drove a team / initiative
Shipped delivered to users
Rebuilt overhauled existing
Reduced cut a cost / time
Increased grew a metric
Designed architected a system
Migrated moved off legacy
Negotiated closed a deal
Automated removed manual toil
Mentored developed people
Scaled grew capacity

Common red flags

  • "Responsible for…", describes a job description, not an accomplishment.
  • "Helped / assisted / worked on…", invisible contribution. Be specific.
  • Adjective stacks, "passionate, hard-working, detail-oriented."
  • No numbers, every bullet should have at least one metric.
  • Over 30 words, recruiters skim. Cut sub-clauses.
