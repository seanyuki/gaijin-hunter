Common questions

Quick answers. Click for the deep dives.

Do I need to speak Japanese to work in Japan?

It depends on the role. Software engineering, design at international companies, and some finance roles operate entirely in English. Marketing, sales, customer support, and most roles at domestic Japanese companies need at least N2. Read the JLPT-by-role guide →

What visa do I need to work in Japan?

The default for most foreigners is the Engineer / Specialist in Humanities visa, which requires a bachelor's degree (or 10+ years of experience). The Highly Skilled Professional visa unlocks fast-track PR if you score 70+ points. Run the eligibility checker →

Can I apply for Japan jobs from abroad?

Yes, most large Japanese tech companies (Mercari, SmartNews, PayPay, LINE Yahoo, Indeed Tokyo) and FAANG offices accept overseas applications and sponsor visas + relocation. Filter the board for Apply from abroad.

How do I write a Japanese resume?

Japan uses two documents: the rirekisho (personal-history form) and the shokumu keirekisho (career history). Both are different from a Western CV. We built free builders for both, fill in English, export Japanese. Open the resume builders →
